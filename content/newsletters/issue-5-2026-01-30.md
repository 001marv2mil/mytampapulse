### Recap + Teaser

So. Gasparilla. Let's talk about it. Jose Gasparilla II sailed into Hillsborough Bay on Friday morning and Mayor Jane Castor surrendered the key to the city like she does every year. The parade rolled 4.5 miles along Bayshore from 2 to 6 PM. 140 units. 300,000+ people. $20 million in economic impact. All of that is normal. What was NOT normal was the weather. Low 50s for the invasion, low 40s by parade start, and upper 30s by the time it wrapped. Wind gusts hitting 40 mph. Pirates were out there shivering in costume holding beads with numb fingers. It was chaos and honestly kind of legendary. If you survived it, you earned it. Looking ahead. Super Bowl LX is next Sunday. Patriots vs Seahawks. Bad Bunny at halftime. We've got watch party spots for you below.

### Community Pick of the Week

> **The Gasparilla Riverwalk Clean Up Volunteers**
>
> Here's the thing about Gasparilla. 300,000 people show up on Friday, party along Bayshore for hours, and then go home. The Riverwalk and surrounding streets look like a war zone. Beads everywhere. Cups. Random shoes. It's a mess. And every single year, a group of people shows up at 8 AM on Sunday morning to clean it all up. Volunteers. Regular people who set an alarm the morning after one of the biggest parties in the country because they care about their city more than they care about sleeping in.
>
> Nobody asks them to be there. There's no clout in it. No Instagram moment. Just people in gloves picking up trash because someone has to and they decided it would be them. That's the part of Gasparilla nobody talks about. The people who love Tampa enough to clean up after it.
>
> Gasparilla Riverwalk Clean Up, Sunday Feb 1, 8 to 11 AM.
>
> *The kind of people who make Tampa better than it was yesterday.*
