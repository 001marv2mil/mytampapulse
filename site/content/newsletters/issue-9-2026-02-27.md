### Pro Tip

> *The Gasparilla Festival of the Arts is FREE but parking will test your patience. Julian B Lane Riverfront Park is right on the Riverwalk so here's the move. Park at one of the garages downtown (Pam Iorio or Fort Brooke) and walk or scooter over along the Riverwalk. You'll avoid the lot chaos entirely and honestly the walk is half the experience. Over 100,000 people come through this thing. Get there early Saturday before 11 AM if you want to browse without bumping elbows the whole time.*

### Hidden Gems

- 🏛️ **Henry B. Plant Museum** at the University of Tampa. This building is a National Historic Landmark. Moorish Revival architecture from 1891. It was Tampa's original grand hotel before it was a university. Most people drive past those silver minarets every day and have no idea what's inside. Go find out.
- 🌿 **Weedon Island Preserve** is in its prime right now. Best visited October through April and we're right in the sweet spot. Kayaking, hiking, a cultural center. One of the most underrated outdoor spots in the entire bay area. Seriously.
- 🎭 **Tampa's Black History Museum** 5th annual reception Thu 2/27. A piece of Tampa's history that doesn't get enough attention. Worth your time.
- 🍝 **J's Asian Kitchen** just opened. Japanese, Korean, Thai, Vietnamese all under one roof. When you can't decide what you want, this is the answer.
- 🎸 **Tusk (Fleetwood Mac tribute)** at Capitol Theatre Sat 2/28. If you missed Fleetwood Mac, this is the closest you'll get. Capitol Theatre is the perfect room for it too.

### Community Pick of the Week

> **The Gasparilla Festival of the Arts Volunteer Organizers**
>
> Here's something most people don't think about when they walk through the Gasparilla Festival of the Arts. This thing is free. Completely free. Over 100,000 people show up across two days to see 250+ juried artists, hear live music on two stages, and walk along the Riverwalk surrounded by art. And nobody pays a dime to get in. That doesn't happen by accident. Behind this festival is a small army of volunteer organizers who have been making it work for 56 years. Fifty-six years of coordinating artists from across the country, staging live music, managing logistics for a crowd that fills Julian B Lane Riverfront Park to capacity. All so Tampa gets a free world-class art festival every year.
>
> These aren't event industry professionals. They're Tampa people. People who believe that art should be accessible and that the Riverwalk should be for everyone. They handle the $90K in artist awards, the jury process, the vendor logistics, the music booking. They do it because they care about this city's cultural identity.
>
> Gasparilla Festival of the Arts, Feb 28-Mar 1 at Julian B Lane Riverfront Park.
>
> *The kind of people who make sure the best things in Tampa stay free.*
