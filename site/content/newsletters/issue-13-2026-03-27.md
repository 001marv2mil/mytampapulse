### 🎯 Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **The Landon** (717 S Howard Ave) just opened in the old 717 South space. Chef Robert Hesse from Hell's Kitchen is running the show. Make a reservation. This one's going to book up fast.
- **Tampa Food Truck Central** on E. Broadway is the move nobody's talking about yet. Colombian-Venezuelan fusion from Sancocho y Lena is the standout. Open till 10 PM most nights.
- If you're doing **Sugar Sand Festival**, go on a weekday evening. Weekends are wall-to-wall families. Tuesday is senior discount day so it's quieter too.
- **Busch Gardens Food, Wine & Garden Festival** has free concerts every weekend through May. Warren G on Friday, Dylan Scott Saturday, KANSAS Sunday. All free with park admission. That's a cheat code.
- The **Ybor City Saturday Market** at Centennial Park is a different vibe than the big festivals. Go early (9 AM), grab coffee from one of the local vendors, and just walk. It's lowkey one of the best Saturday morning moves in Tampa.
- **Jimmy's Tacos** on Central Ave in St. Pete is doing birria and shrimp tacos on handmade tortillas. No line yet. That won't last.

### 📋 Digest

- 🔥 **Pride of Tampa** launches its inaugural celebration this week in Ybor. Opening party Thursday, free festival Saturday at the Cuban Club with hourly drag shows noon to 5 PM, closing party Saturday night at 1920 Ybor
- 🥪 **International Cuban Sandwich Festival** returns Sunday to Centennial Park. They're attempting a 370-foot Cuban sandwich for the world record. Free admission, live salsa, and a legit vendor competition
- 🎨 **Mainsail Art Festival** brings 250+ juried artists to Vinoy Park in St. Pete this Saturday-Sunday. 51 years running. Free admission and $60K in prizes. The art is genuinely impressive
- 🍽️ **Oro** is opening at Rome Collective this month. Spanish restaurant doing wood-fired paella and tapas. Keep an eye on this one
- 🎵 **Into the Woods** at American Stage is doing outdoor performances at Demens Landing in St. Pete. Sondheim under the stars, $40-60. Runs through April 26
- 🌮 **SoDough Square** in St. Pete is the new Detroit-style pizza spot on 4th St N. Wings and fried dough too. Not a lot of people know about it yet
- 🎪 **Asian Pacific Islander Cultural Festival** at Curtis Hixon Saturday, noon to 7 PM. Live cultural performances, free admission. The kind of thing that's easy to walk past and regret later

### 💎 Hidden Gems

- 🗝️ **Trailer Daddy**, Central Ave, St. Pete. New trailer-park-themed cocktail bar. Sounds ridiculous. The drinks are actually really well done. Go with zero expectations and you'll leave impressed.
- 🍳 **Tampa Food Truck Central**, 4908 E. Broadway, Tampa. Brand new food truck park that quietly soft-launched. Five vendors including Sancocho y Lena doing Colombian-Venezuelan street food. Open late on weekends.
- 🛍️ **Secondhand Market at St. Pete Youth Farm**, St. Pete. Saturday mornings, 9 AM to noon. Thrift finds at a literal youth farm. The kind of thing you stumble into and tell everyone about later.
- 🎨 **Tempus Projects: "Awkward Transactions"**, Ybor City. New exhibit opening Thursday April 2, 7-9 PM. Free gallery night. Tempus always does weird, interesting stuff. This is the Ybor art scene at its best.
- 🍕 **Smith's Provisions**, Sparkman Wharf. New roadside burger stand concept right on the water. Old-school vibes, simple menu. Not trying to be fancy and that's the whole point.

### ⭐ Community Pick: Chef Suzanne "Suz" Lara of LARA

> You walk into LARA on 7th Ave in Ybor and you immediately know somebody specific built this. It's 52 seats. It's a gastropub crossed with an apothecary bar. The menu has Korean drunk eggs next to Filipino Loganisa hot dogs next to chicken wings roasted over a Japanese charcoal grill. None of that should work together. All of it does.
>
> That's because Suz Lara built this place the way she thinks about food. She grew up around Latin, Asian, and Italian kitchens and instead of picking one lane she said I'm taking all of them. The cocktails are the same way. Herbal, layered, unexpected. You order something and it tastes like nothing you've had at any other bar in Tampa.
>
> People in Ybor talk about LARA like it's their secret. They text their friends about it. They bring dates there. They go on a random Tuesday and sit at the bar and just let Suz's team surprise them. The place just celebrated its first anniversary and gave away free beer and tacos. That tells you everything about the vibe.
>
> 1919 E 7th Ave, Ybor City.
>
> *The kind of chef who doesn't need a bigger room. She just needs the right one.*
