### 🎯 Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **Children's Gasparilla** has an air invasion with aerobatic teams before the parade. Most people miss it because they show up late. Don't be most people. Get there early and look up.
- **Kahwa Coffee** at 2802 W Kennedy Blvd now has a drive-thru. Three new matcha drinks just dropped. If you're a matcha person, this is going to be your new daily stop.
- **LARA in Ybor** is doing free beer and free tacos for their first anniversary. That's not a typo. Korean drunk eggs and Filipino hot dogs at an apothecary bar. Go experience it before the secret gets out.

### 💎 Hidden Gems

- 🗝️ **Bookends Fantasy Book Fair**, Corner Club Tampa, Seminole Heights. This one's genuinely wild. Tarot readings, typewriter poetry on demand, a sunset harp serenade, and a secret fairy hunt. Not your average book event. Go with an open mind and you'll leave talking about it for weeks.
- 🍳 **LARA**, Ybor City. Korean drunk eggs. Filipino hot dogs. An apothecary bar. If you haven't been here yet, their first anniversary party this week is your excuse. The vibe is unlike anything else in Ybor.
- 🥐 **Point Chaud**, St. Pete. French crepes and Lavazza coffee in a spot that feels like it was teleported from Paris. Lowkey one of the best breakfast moves on the other side of the bridge.
- 😂 **The Commodore**, Ybor/Tampa Heights border. Improv comedy in a former pool hall. The kind of place where you show up not knowing what to expect and leave crying laughing. Thursday nights hit different.
- 🍞 **The Crumb Factory Bakery**, Dunedin. New location just opened. Artisanal sourdough, French pastries, and a "flour salt water time" philosophy that you can taste in every loaf.

### ⭐ Community Pick of the Week

> **The Crumb Factory Bakery Founders**, Dunedin
>
> Here's what I love about Tampa Bay's food scene. It's people who genuinely care about what they make. The folks behind The Crumb Factory Bakery have been doing naturally leavened sourdough in St. Pete, and this week they expanded to Dunedin with a brand new location. Their whole thing is simple. Flour, salt, water, time. That's it. No shortcuts, no preservatives, just patience and craft. The kind of people who wake up at 3 AM because the dough tells them to.
>
> People have been driving across the bridge for their bread. Not because of marketing or hype. Because someone handed them a loaf and they couldn't stop thinking about it. That's how it spreads. One loaf at a time, one friend texting another "you need to try this."
>
> The new Dunedin location opened January 24th. Sourdough, French pastries, the works.
>
> *The kind of people who remind you that the best things are made slowly.*
