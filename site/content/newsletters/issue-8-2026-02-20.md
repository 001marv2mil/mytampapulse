### Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **Cigar City Tattoo Fest** is $25 for a single day, $60 for the weekend. But here's the move. Sunday has a FREE Punk Rock Flea Market on the Cuban Club patio from 12 to 6 PM. You don't need a tattoo fest ticket to hit the flea market. Vinyl, patches, local art, weird stuff. It's one of those things you walk into with no plan and leave with a bag full of things you didn't know you needed.
- **Fiesta Day** on 7th Ave is the real deal. 79 years running. Five blocks celebrating Cuban, Italian, Spanish, Jewish, and German heritage. It's free. It's all day. The food alone is worth the trip. Go hungry.
- **Smith's Provisions and The Back Bar** are both announced for Sparkman Wharf under Cru Hospitality Group. No opening date yet but keep your eyes on this. The waterfront dining lineup is getting serious.
- **Free yoga on the Riverwalk** happens every Sunday from 6 to 7 PM. If you need to decompress after a week like this one, that's your move. Show up with a mat and nothing else.
- That **smash burger spot** from Tampa brothers Ahmed and Ali Mansour is coming. Lebanese-inspired chicken sandwich and a falafel veggie smash burger. I don't have a date yet but I'm already in line mentally.
- **Deviant Libation** does a "Sunday Service" with live music for $5. Lowkey one of the best value hangs in town.

### Community Pick of the Week

> **The Cigar City Tattoo Fest Organizers at the Cuban Club**
>
> There's something about bringing 100+ tattoo artists into a building that's been standing since 1917 that just hits different. The people behind Cigar City Tattoo Fest didn't pick the Cuban Club by accident. Ybor City has always been the part of Tampa where creative people do creative things. The cigar workers, the musicians, the artists. It's been that way for over a century. And now you've got tattooers from all over the country setting up inside a building that was originally built as a gathering place for the Cuban community. That's not a coincidence. That's respect for what Ybor has always been.
>
> The organizers could have booked a convention center. They could have gone sterile and corporate. Instead they leaned into the history. Friday night kicks off with an art showcase at The Bricks Ybor. Sunday wraps with a free Punk Rock Flea Market on the patio. They built a weekend that feels like it belongs in Ybor because they understand what makes that neighborhood special in the first place.
>
> Cigar City Tattoo Fest, Feb 20-22 at the Cuban Club, Ybor City.
>
> *The kind of people who get what Ybor is really about.*
