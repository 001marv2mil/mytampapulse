### Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **Sant' Yago pre-party at Carmine's** is Thursday night (Feb 13). If you want the Ybor energy without the full parade crowd, this is lowkey the move. Get there early.
- **State Fair food bucket list** before it closes Sunday. The deep-fried Cuban burrito is exactly what it sounds like and it works. The Reese's Pieces donut burger is unhinged. The Dubai chocolate funnel cake is new this year. Go hungry.
- **J's Asian Kitchen** just opened at 5229 N Florida Ave. Japanese, Korean, Thai, Vietnamese. All under one roof. This stretch of Florida Ave is becoming a serious food corridor and I'm here for it.
- **Con Amor** is now open in Seminole Heights. Modern Mexican with house-nixtamalized masa. If you missed the opening week buzz, go now before the wait times get absurd.
- **Valentine's Day without reservations?** Armature Works is doing a free outdoor movie. "Crazy, Stupid, Love" on the lawn with free champagne. Low effort, high reward. Trust me on this one.
- **Portugal. The Man** at Jannus Live Saturday night. If you've never seen a show at Jannus, this is a great first one. Open air, downtown St. Pete, good vibes.

### Hidden Gems

- 🏮 **Lunar Lantern Festival**, Dade City. Yeah it's 45 minutes away. I don't care. Thousands of biodegradable sky lanterns rising into the night sky on Valentine's weekend with 75+ food vendors is worth the drive. This is one of those "how did you even know about this" things.
- 🛍️ **Localtopia**, St. Pete. 300+ local-only vendors, 70+ food trucks, and free SunRunner rides to get there. Zero chains allowed. If you want to support Tampa Bay small businesses and eat your way through a festival at the same time, this is the one.
- 🎥 **Armature Works Valentine's Movie Night**. Free outdoor screening of "Crazy, Stupid, Love" with free champagne on the lawn. This is the cheat code for Valentine's Day if you don't have a plan yet.
- 🏎️ **Festivals of Speed**, Vinoy Park. 250+ exotic cars on display in one of the prettiest parks in St. Pete. This doesn't get a ton of press but car people go nuts for it. $30.
- ⛵ **St. Pete Yacht Club Sailing Regatta**. 225 boats racing in the bay. Free to watch from shore. Grab a coffee, sit on the waterfront, and watch sailboats do their thing. Lowkey one of the best free activities this weekend.

### Community Pick: Carlos "Carlito" Fuente Jr. of Arturo Fuente Cigars

> Carlito Fuente Jr. doesn't just make cigars. He carries Ybor City on his back. Third generation. His grandfather, Arturo, started rolling cigars in Tampa in 1912. His father built Arturo Fuente into one of the most respected names in the world. And Carlito? He's the one who kept it going. Kept it in the family. Kept it connected to Tampa when every business advisor on the planet was probably telling him to move production somewhere cheaper. He didn't. Because Ybor isn't just where the business is. It's who they are.
>
> If you've spent any time in Ybor, you've heard the Fuente name. People don't just respect the cigars. They respect what the family represents. Three generations of Tampa roots. A commitment to craft that hasn't wavered. When people talk about Ybor City being special, this is the kind of family they're talking about. The ones who stayed.
>
> Carlito is the Grand Marshal of the Sant' Yago Knight Parade this Friday, leading 100+ organizations down 7th Ave.
>
> *The kind of person whose family didn't just build a business in Tampa. They built Tampa.*
