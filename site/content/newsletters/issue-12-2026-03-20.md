### Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **The Landon** (717 S Howard Ave) is going to be hard to get into fast. Chef Robert Hesse is doing birria dumplings and elevated croquetas. Make a reservation now. Not next week. Now.
- **DroneArt Show** is one of the coolest things I've seen in Tampa in a while. Live string quartet playing Vivaldi while drones paint the sky above you. Thursday and Friday nights at 8:45 PM. The $29.90 GA tickets are all you need.
- If you're hitting **Hyde Park Art Festival** on Saturday, go early. By 1 PM that village gets packed. Morning is when you can actually browse without bumping into a stroller every three feet.
- **Busch Gardens Food Wine & Garden Festival** is doing free concerts every weekend. Skillet on Saturday, Robin Thicke on Sunday. All free with park admission. Still one of the best deals in Tampa.
- **Pinellas Farmers & Flea Market** at Derby Lane has 150+ vendors and it's free. The old Wagon Wheel crowd migrated here. Saturday morning, show up early, bring cash.
- The **Rays** open their season Thursday at St. Louis. Set your alerts. It's going to be a long summer of baseball and I'm ready.

### Hidden Gems

- 🎆 **DroneArt Show**, Florida State Fairgrounds, Thu-Fri 8:45 PM. I keep bringing this up because it's genuinely unlike anything Tampa normally gets. Live Vivaldi and Tchaikovsky with synchronized drones overhead. Go see it.
- 🛍️ **Pinellas Farmers & Flea Market**, Derby Lane. 150+ vendors, free admission. The old Wagon Wheel crowd found a new home here and it's thriving. Saturday mornings are the move. Tampa Bay Times just featured it this week.
- 🏓 **Pete Athletic Paddle & Social**, The Factory, St. Pete. 14 pickleball courts plus 3 bars. Whether you play or just want to drink and watch people miss easy shots, it works either way.
- 🌎 **Que Es Colombia Festival**, Mar 22. Cultural fest that most people don't know about. Colombian food, music, dance. The kind of thing you go to once and then tell everyone about.
- 🍹 **Trailer Daddy**, Central Ave, St. Pete. 5,000 sq ft retro trailer park bar that opened earlier this month. Lawn flamingos, neon signs, cocktails served in flamingo glasses. Sounds gimmicky. It's actually a really good time.

### Community Pick of the Week

> **Michael Stewart, Owner of The Landon**
>
> If you've driven down South Howard in the last year and a half, you've seen the empty space at 717 South. The old 717 South restaurant was a SoHo staple for years before a fire shut it down in 2024. It sat vacant. People walked past it and wondered if anything would ever go in there again.
>
> Michael Stewart decided something would. He took on the space, brought in Chef Robert Hesse, and rebuilt what a lot of people thought was gone for good. He named the restaurant after his son, Landon. That tells you everything you need to know about what this place means to him.
>
> The Landon opened March 23 and it's already clear this isn't just another restaurant filling a vacancy. It's someone putting their name and their family's name on something because they believe in it. New American fine dining with birria dumplings and elevated croquetas that Chef Hesse is making his own.
>
> 717 S Howard Ave, Tampa.
>
> *The kind of person who sees something broken and decides to fix it. Welcome back, 717.*
