### 🎯 Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **BTS at Raymond James** Saturday + Sunday, both nights at 8:00 PM. Two-night residency. The biggest pop tour in the world is in your backyard. If you didn't get tickets, the energy outside the gates is going to be wild on its own.
- **Florence + The Machine** at Benchmark Tuesday April 28, 7:30 PM. The 'Everybody Scream Tour.' If you saw her on the High as Hope tour you already know — she does *not* miss live.
- **Tampa Bay Black Heritage Festival** Saturday + Sunday at Curtis Hixon Park. Free. Music, food, vendors, full family vibe. Easy parking weekend if you hit the Riverwalk garages early.
- **Bowling For Soup** at Busch Gardens Friday April 24, 8:00 PM. Festival ticket gets you the show. 1,000% will play 1985.
- **The Beach Boys** at Busch Gardens Sunday April 26, 6:00 PM. Wholesome closing act for the Food and Wine Festival's final weekend. Bring sunscreen.
- **Stereophonic** at Straz Tuesday through Thursday, 7:30 PM. Won five Tonys including Best Play. About a band recording an album in the 70s and watching it eat them alive. Smaller room — book early.

### 💎 Hidden Gems

- 🗝️ **Con Amor — new Seminole Heights Mexican at Avenue Lofts** — Oaxacan marinated steak, tetelas, grilled fish, chicken mole. Real menu, not the same Tex-Mex you've had eight times.
- 🍳 **La Central — family-owned tacos in the old Wu spot on N. Florida Ave** — fire-grilled tacos, fajitas, legit corner-spot energy. Walk-in friendly.
- 🛍️ **Nori Nori hand rolls in Seminole Heights** — counter-only, fresh-fresh sushi, the kind of place you find once and gatekeep from the group chat.
- 🎨 **Mat Pilates with Rare Body on the Riverwalk Saturday 9-10AM** — free or low-cost outdoor class on the water. Bring your own mat.
- 🍕 **Art on the Spectrum at the Riverwalk Sunday afternoon** — community art event, family-friendly, the kind of Tampa thing only locals know is happening.

### ⭐ Community Pick

> This week's Community Pick is **the new Seminole Heights food hall at 4205 N Florida Ave**. Eight or nine concepts under one roof — Korean street food, Japanese fusion, Vietnamese, American smashburgers, wood-fired pizza. Seminole Heights has been quietly turning into Tampa's most interesting food neighborhood for five years now and this is the move that locks it in.
