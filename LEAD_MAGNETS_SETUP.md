# Tampa Pulse Lead Magnets — Setup Guide

These 6 lead magnets will drive referrals and giveaway entries. Start with 2-3, then rotate every 3-4 months.

---

## Lead Magnet #1: Hidden Gems Database (HIGHEST IMPACT)

**What it is:** Airtable database of 50+ verified Tampa hidden gems
**Why it works:** Ben's Bites drove 30K+ subscribers with a link database
**Time to create:** 2-3 hours

### Setup Steps:

1. **Create Airtable base** (free tier works)
   - Table name: "Tampa Hidden Gems"
   - Fields:
     - Name (text)
     - Neighborhood (select: Ybor, SoHo, Channelside, Hyde Park, etc.)
     - Category (select: Bar, Restaurant, Cafe, Shop, Activity)
     - Vibe (text: "lowkey dive," "upscale cocktails," "date night," etc.)
     - Address (text)
     - Price Range (select: $, $$, $$$, $$$$)
     - Photos (attachments) — add 1-2 photos per spot
     - Notes (text) — 1-2 sentence description in Marv voice
     - Why it's hidden (text) — why locals love it, why tourists miss it

2. **Populate with 50+ spots**
   - Use your existing newsletter issues (go back through past issues)
   - Fill in spots mentioned in: hidden gems section, community picks, digest
   - Add photos from Unsplash or take your own

3. **Share the base**
   - Click "Share" in Airtable
   - Select "Create a shared link to form" or "Create a shared link to view"
   - Get the public link (e.g., `airtable.com/shr/xxxxx`)

4. **Add to referral program**
   - In beehiiv/SparkLoop: Set this link as the referral reward
   - Copy: _"Get instant access to 50+ Tampa hidden gems that tourists never find — organized by neighborhood, vibe, and price. Locals only."_

## Lead Magnet #2: Event Calendar PDF

**What it is:** 60-day event calendar in PDF form
**Why it works:** Easy to download, shareable, people actually save it
**Time to create:** 1-2 hours per month (then automated)

### Setup:

1. **Create a Google Doc template** (easier than PDF software)
   - Title: "Complete Tampa Event Calendar — Next 60 Days"
   - Headers by week: "March 27 - April 2," "April 3 - April 9," etc.
   - List events by date with: Event name, Date/Time, Location, Vibe tag (#Free, #Paid, #Music, #Food)
   - Use your existing event data from newsletter parser

2. **Update monthly**
   - Pull upcoming events from your newsletter content
   - Add new discoveries
   - Export as PDF

3. **Host the PDF**
   - Upload to Google Drive
   - Get shareable link (anyone with link can view)
   - Shorten with bit.ly: `bit.ly/tampa-events-60`

4. **Add to referral/giveaway**
   - Link in beehiiv: "Get the complete 60-day event calendar"

## Lead Magnet #3: Neighborhood Guide (PDF)

**What it is:** "Where to Actually Go" guide — 25+ neighborhoods
**Why it works:** People search "best neighborhoods in Tampa" — this is the answer
**Time to create:** 3-4 hours

### Structure:

```
TAMPA NEIGHBORHOODS GUIDE — WHERE TO ACTUALLY GO

1. YBOR CITY
   What: Historic, nightlife, restaurants, shops
   Where to eat: La Creperie, Bricks of Ybor, Columbia
   Why go: Friday/Saturday nights, anniversary dinners, history
   Vibe: Touristy but fun, packed on weekends
   Skip if: You hate crowds

2. SOHO (South Howard)
   What: Brewery district, art, young crowd
   Where to eat: Glazed & Confused, Oxalis, The Bricks
   Why go: Live music, craft beer, weekend scene
   Vibe: Instagram-friendly, casual
   Best for: First date, happy hour with friends

3. CHANNELSIDE
   What: Waterfront, dining, entertainment
   ...

[Repeat for 20+ neighborhoods]
```

### How to populate:
- Go through your last 13 newsletter issues
- Extract neighborhoods mentioned in: digest, events, hidden gems, community picks
- Add 2-3 sentences per neighborhood: what's there, where to eat, why go
- Use Marv's voice: casual, opinionated, honest

### Tools:
- Write in Google Docs, export as PDF
- Or use Canva (has PDF export)
- Upload to Google Drive, get shareable link

## Lead Magnet #4: Restaurant Database (Airtable)

**What it is:** 75+ Tampa restaurants organized by neighborhood & vibe
**Why it works:** People constantly ask "where should we eat?" — this is the answer
**Time to create:** 3-4 hours

### Airtable fields:
- Restaurant Name
- Neighborhood (linked to Neighborhood Gems table)
- Cuisine (select: Italian, Thai, Cuban, etc.)
- Vibe (select: Date night, casual lunch, trendy, hidden gem)
- Price Range ($ to $$$$)
- Best for (text: "first dates", "solo lunch", "group hangout")
- Address & Link
- Photos (2-3 per restaurant)
- Notes (1-2 sentences, Marv voice)
- Open: Yes/No (so you can hide permanently closed spots)

### Where to get data:
- Your newsletter mentions (past 13 issues)
- Google Maps (search "best restaurants Tampa")
- Instagram location tags
- Local blogs
