### Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **Reggae Rise Up** is a four-day festival and it's massive. If you're only going one day, Saturday is the move. Slightly Stoopid and Sublime are both playing and the energy peaks. Bring cash for food vendors. The lines for card-only are brutal.
- **The Great Junk Hunt** at the State Fairgrounds is 87,000 square feet of curated vintage and it's been named a top-15 flea market in the country. Go Thursday when it opens if you want first picks. Friday is for the casual browsers.
- **Paddy Fest** in St. Pete at Williams Park runs Thursday through Saturday. Celtic music all weekend, Irish food, good energy. It's smaller and more chill than the Tampa stuff if that's your vibe.
- **Busch Gardens** is doing a St. Patrick's Day Celebration Mar 15-17. Irish dancing, a Leprechaun character wandering around, and corned beef specials. It's cheesy in the best way.
- **Dueling Pianos** at Grand Central Brewhouse is free. Every time I mention this people are surprised it exists. Just go. It's a great time and you don't spend a dime to get in.
- **Seminole Heights** continues to be the neighborhood that quietly has everything. Hit Mekenita Cantina for tacos and then walk to Cigar City Brewing's taproom. That's a perfect afternoon right there.

### Hidden Gems

- 🗝️ **Parque Amigos de Jose Marti** in Ybor City. This tiny park on 8th Ave was once officially Cuban sovereign land. There's a bust of Jose Marti and a flagpole that used to fly the Cuban flag. Most people in Tampa have never heard of it. Walk through it next time you're in Ybor and read the plaques. It's a piece of Tampa history hiding in plain sight.
- 🛍️ **The Great Junk Hunt**, State Fairgrounds. I know it's in the main events but it deserves a spot here too. 87,000 square feet of curated vintage. Furniture, art, jewelry, weird stuff you didn't know you needed. This isn't your average flea market.
- 🛒 **Water Street Sunday Market** Mar 15. 60+ vendors right in Water Street. Produce, handmade goods, food vendors. Go early for the best picks, stay for the waterfront vibes.
- 🍺 **Seminole Heights food crawl.** Mekenita Cantina for the tacos. Cigar City Brewing taproom for the beer. Walk between them. The whole neighborhood is underrated and I will keep saying this until everyone agrees with me.
- 🍹 **Trailer Daddy** on Central Ave in St. Pete. Retro trailer park themed bar. Sounds gimmicky but the cocktails are legitimately good. One of those spots where the concept is fun and the execution actually backs it up.

### Community Pick of the Week

> **The River O' Green Volunteers**
>
> Every year a group of volunteers and organizers does something that sounds completely made up. They dye the Hillsborough River green. Not a little green. Full on emerald green running right through downtown Tampa. It's become one of those traditions that people plan their whole weekend around and it wouldn't happen without the crew that makes it work.
>
> These are the folks who show up before sunrise on Saturday to prep. The ones coordinating with the city, managing the eco-safe dye, setting up the festival at Curtis Hixon, and making sure the Rough Riders parade rolls at 2 PM like clockwork. They don't do it for credit. Most people watching from the Riverwalk have no idea who's behind it. They just see a green river and a good time.
>
> What makes it special is that it started as a small St. Patrick's Day thing and grew into a full Tampa tradition. The kind of thing that makes you stop on the bridge and pull out your phone. The kind of thing visitors don't believe until they see it. And it happens because a group of people love this city enough to turn the river green every March.
>
> River O' Green Fest, Saturday March 14 at Curtis Hixon Waterfront Park.
>
> *The kind of people who make Tampa feel like Tampa.*
