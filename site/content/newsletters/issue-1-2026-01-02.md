### Marv's Tampa Pro Tips

- **Ringling Bros** is only here through Saturday. If you're taking kids, go Thursday night. Way less crowded than the weekend shows and same 65 performers from 17 countries. The arena setup is actually impressive.
- **Treehouse BBQ** in NE St. Pete is doing a soft opening and the brisket is smoked 12-16 hours. Texas-style, no shortcuts. Get there before the word spreads because once Tampa BBQ Twitter finds this it's over.
- The **Ybor City Saturday Market** at Centennial Park is one of the most underrated Saturday morning moves in Tampa. Show up around 9 AM, grab coffee from a local vendor, and just wander. It's lowkey great.
- **Madame Fortune** is a speakeasy tucked behind Roast on 7th in Ybor. No big sign, no Instagram hype yet. Just good cocktails and a vibe that feels like you found something you weren't supposed to. Go on a weeknight.
- **La Segunda Central Bakery** has been making Cuban bread since 1915. If you haven't been, just go. Get a Cuban toast and cafe con leche. It's not fancy. It's perfect.
- If you're hitting the **Bucs vs Panthers** game Friday, bring a poncho. It's going to rain and Raymond James doesn't care about your outfit. Spoiler if you're reading this late: Bucs won 16-14 in a wet one.

### Community Pick of the Week

**Community Pick: The People Behind Crowbar**

Here's the thing about Crowbar. It's not just a bar. It's Ybor's last full-time live music venue. And they just announced that 2026 is their final year.

Let that sit for a second. Every band that came through Tampa on the way up played Crowbar. Every weird local act that needed a stage got one there. The people who ran that room didn't do it because it was profitable. They did it because Ybor without live music isn't really Ybor. The staff, the bookers, the sound engineers. They kept that place alive through everything. Recessions, the pandemic, all of it. They showed up because the music mattered more than the money.

If you talk to anyone who's been in Tampa's music scene for more than a few years, Crowbar comes up in the first five minutes. It's the venue people tell stories about. The place where you saw a band in a room of 50 people and then watched them sell out arenas two years later. The kind of spot that made you feel like you were part of something.

Crowbar is at 1812 N 17th St in Ybor City. The "Beginning of the End" concerts run January 2-3 and there will be shows throughout the year.

*The kind of place that made Tampa feel like Tampa. And the kind of people who made sure it stayed that way.*
